Famous places to visit in Yercaud

---------- ----------

A trip from Salem to Yercaud sounds fantastic! Yercaud is a beautiful hill station in Tamil Nadu, known for its scenic views, pleasant climate, and serene atmosphere. It's located about 30 km from Salem and is a popular getaway spot. With our Yercaud tour package from Salem best time to visit Yercaud is between October and June when the weather is pleasant. It tends to get a little chilly in the evenings and early mornings, so packing a light sweater or jacket is a good idea.


Kiliyur Falls

Kiliyur Falls is a waterfall on the Periyaaru river in the Servarayan hill range in the Eastern Ghats, Tamil nadu, India. The waters overflowing the Yercaud Lake fall 91 m (300 ft) into the Kiliyur Valley. People need to walk down 200–250 steep steps from the nearest road to reach this waterfall.


Ladies & Gents Seats

This Lady's Seat is a very poorly maintained place by the local administration. It is surrounded and encroached by vendors and is full of garbage, One of the best view point in Yercaud.


Servarayan Hills

Perched at a height of 1620 m near the Salem district of Tamil Nadu in Yercaud, the Shevaroy Hills are a popular tourist spot in the region. Boasting of magnificent views of lush green meadows, towering trees, dense woods and mighty hills, the hills were formerly known as Servarayan Hills which later got anglicized to ‘Shevaroy’.


Pakoda View Point

Pagoda Point in Yercaud is a popular viewpoint, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding hills, valleys, and the distant plains of Salem. The point is named after a small pagoda-shaped structure located at the top, which is believed to have been built by the local tribes as a way to mark the location for navigation.

It's a peaceful and scenic spot, ideal for a relaxing time surrounded by nature. The view at sunrise and sunset is particularly beautiful, with the changing colors of the sky and the landscape creating a serene atmosphere. The place also has a calm, cool breeze, making it a perfect spot to unwind and take in the beauty of the Western Ghats.
